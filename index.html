Why Nest exists

It was a Sunday afternoon. My husband had taken the kids swimming, the sun was pouring through the windows, and the house was just — quiet. The kind of quiet you forget exists.

I'd been taking a break from social media. And somewhere in that stillness I noticed something. The pressure I'd been carrying — the one that turned ordinary moments into content — had lifted. I felt lighter. More like myself. I realized how much of my anxiety hadn't even been mine. It had come from watching other people's lives.

So I started thinking about what it would look like if it didn't have to be that way. I spent two years thinking about what was missing before I built a single screen — because I didn't want to build another version of the thing that broke it. I wanted something different at every level.

That was the idea. On a sofa. In the sun. On a Sunday.

I called it Nest. Because every mom deserves a place to land.

Steph  —  Founder of Nest
